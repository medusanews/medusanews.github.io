Adnexitis
Inflammation of the ovaries and uterine (fallopian) tubes called adneksita or salpingooforita. This is one of the most common gynecological diseases, mostly found in women aged 20 to 30 years. Adnexitis brings a lot of inconvenience, may be hiding under the guise of other diseases and, most importantly, a risk factor for infertility. It is therefore necessary to know the symptoms of this disease, to promptly seek medical attention.

Atrophic vaginitis
Atrophic vaginitis - a set of trophic disorders of the mucous membrane of the vagina associated with a deficiency of sex hormones in menopause. The basis of treatment is hormone replacement therapy.

Barrier methods of contraception
Principles of barrier contraceptives is to block the penetration of sperm in the cervical mucus. The benefits of barrier methods of contraception are: they apply and are valid only locally, without causing systemic change, and they have few side effects, they are largely protect against diseases, sexually transmitted diseases, with virtually no contraindications for use, in addition, they do not require the use of a trained medical the staff.

Planning for pregnancy
To prepare for a future pregnancy should be to create the most favorable conditions for the conception and successful course of this difficult period in the life of every family. Late toxemia of pregnancy
Late toxemia (preeclampsia) pregnant - is a pathological condition that occurs in the second half of pregnancy and is accompanied by a variety of disorders of the nervous system until the development of seizures, cardiovascular and endocrine systems, hemostasis, and other systems, which may lead to adverse outcomes for mother and fetus. The frequency of 7-16%.

Polyp of the cervix - is local tissue growth cervical canal. Polyps in most cases are benign. However, the diagnosis of cervical polyp is a strong indication for surgical removal and histological study.

Postpartum
Postpartum period includes the time from the birth of the placenta until the regression of changes in the body during pregnancy. This period is 6 weeks.

Premenstrual syndrome
Premenstrual tension syndrome is associated with the consequent fluctuations in hormone levels in the blood. Previously, premenstrual syndrome viewed as a psychological disease, while researchers have not shown that this condition is organic in nature, due to changes in hormone levels in the body.

Early toxicosis during pregnancy
Early morning sickness usually observed in the I trimester. The prevalence of early toxicity is so high that very often the symptoms are considered classic signs of pregnancy.

Rh-conflict pregnancy
Rhesus-conflict - hemolytic disorder that can lead to various consequences: from disease of the newborn jaundice and the need to rely on blood transfusions to miscarriages, premature births and stillbirths. Fortunately, new therapies and new drugs greatly reduces the risk.

Childbirth
Childbirth - a physiological process of exile and formed mature fetus from the uterus to the outside. Term labor - these are the families of pregnancy from 38 to 42 weeks. Like all physiological processes, childbirth largely occur instinctively and managed by hormonal and neurological level. Polycystic Ovarian Syndrome
Polycystic Ovarian Syndrome (PCOS), also known as Stein-Leventhal syndrome or sclerocysticum is an endocrine (hormonal) disorder that affects about 5-10% of all women of childbearing age and is a major cause of infertility.

In vitro fertilization - is one of the treatments for infertility, the essence of which is that the egg is fertilized and develop outside the body of the mother. Endometriosis
Endometriosis - a benign disease in which cells of the mucous membrane of the uterus can be detected in other organs / tissues. The process can be genital and pelvic organs include (fallopian tubes, ovaries, ligaments) and / or extragenital, with the involvement of the abdominal cavity, bladder, lung tissue. The disease occurs in 10-15% of women aged 25-44 years.
