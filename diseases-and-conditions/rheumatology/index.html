Ankylosing Spondylitis
Ankylosing spondylitis - a term describing a form of arthritis that mainly affects the joints of the spine. This disease usually affects the small joints between the vertebrae and reduces the mobility of the joints until the formation of ankylosis (bone adhesions with each other).

Arthritis in children
It is hard to imagine that children (even very small ones) may be arthritis. In fact, every 1000th child develop arthritis during the first years of life, which is usually difficult to diagnose. There are several different types of arthritis that occur in childhood.

Paget’s disease
Bone Paget’s disease occurs when the bone is damaged recovery mechanism of the body. Paget’s disease can occur in any bone of the body, but more often involved in the process - the pelvis, spine, skull and long bones of hands and feet. The disease occurs twice as often in men than in women.

Giant cell arteritis
Giant cell arteritis or Horton’s disease - an inflammatory disease of the arteries. The average age of onset - 70 years. 65% of patients - women.

Osteoarthritis
Osteoarthritis - the most common form of arthritis. Nearly half of people older than 60 years and virtually all older than 80 years suffer from osteoarthritis. The disease occurs as a result of mechanical failure of the normal structures of the joint, changes in the capsule and cartilage damage.

Drugs used to enhance bone

The use of glucocorticosteroids (GCS)
Glucocorticosteroids (GCS) - a substance similar to natural steroid hormone that produces the body. Their use reduces pain, redness and swelling in the joints and surrounding tissues. Psoriatic arthritis
Psoriatic arthritis - an inflammation of the joints in psoriasis (chronic skin disease). Psoriatic arthritis develops in approximately 10% of patients with psoriasis. Often it occurs in a few or even many years after the first symptoms of psoriasis, but may appear before the onset of skin symptoms.

Reactive arthritis
Reactive arthritis - is inflammation of the joints that occurs in response to an infectious disease, usually within 2-4 weeks after the onset of infection.

Polymyalgia rheumatica
Polymyalgia rheumatica - the name of the state, which leads to severe morning stiffness and pain in the muscles of the shoulders, neck, spine, hips and thighs. Most often, polymyalgia rheumatica occurs in people older than 50 years. Women suffer from polymyalgia rheumatica is slightly larger than males.

Rheumatoid Arthritis
Rheumatoid arthritis - a chronic systemic autoimmune disease of the connective tissue of nature, which affects mainly the small joints - arthritis develops, leading to permanent joint deformity and disruption of their function. Most often the disease begins between the ages of 35-50 years.

The role of drugs in the treatment of arthritis
The use of drugs is an important part of arthritis treatment, although this is only part of the treatment of these diseases. The success of treatment depends on several factors, including the way of life. At the moment there is no means of guaranteeing cure, so the drugs can only help to cope with the symptoms and slow the progression of the disease.

Systemic lupus erythematosus
Systemic lupus erythematosus - a severe systemic disease of connective tissue immune nature in which the affected skin and various internal organs. 90% of patients were women aged 20-40 years.

Scleroderma
Scleroderma (Scleroderma) - diffuse connective tissue disease, which in some areas of the skin, and sometimes throughout the skin, scar tissue is deposited. With the spread of the scar tissue is formed not only in the skin, but also in the locomotor system, lungs, digestive tract, heart and kidneys.

Regular exercise help strengthen bones, but the choice of exercises must take into account a number of features.

What is osteoporosis?
With age, all people have the bones become less strong. Osteoporosis - a disease in which bones become too thin and fragile, because of what there is a high risk of fractures. It affects more than 50% of women and 30% of men older than 60 years of age, rarely occurs in young people.
