The Degree of Obesity. How to Determine the Degree of Obesity

Jan 21st, 2016 5:16 pm

Obesity is a disease in which the body weight is increased by increasing the content of fatty tissue. Besides the obvious harm to health (people with its presence will develop diabetes, atherosclerosis and other disorders), the disease significantly spoils the appearance, worsening and even disfiguring figure.

Furthermore, the term "obesity" means a pathology, this word is unpleasant, is clearly negative, to some extent condemneth group. Naturally, neither one nor the other owners do not like excess weight, which often prefer to call his lack of completeness, obesity, but not this definition. However, there is a way to dot the "i": some simple calculations help to distinguish the fullness of obesity.

What is BMI and what is it for?

On the body mass index (BMI), we have repeatedly said in other posts, devoted to weight loss. It refers to the ratio of height and weight, which is expressed by a numerical value and gives an indication of normal, insufficient or overweight humans. It calculated it is very simple: the value of the body weight in kilograms divided by the square of height in meters.

Determining the degree of obesity by BMI. Method one

In this case, the degree is determined depending on the body mass index.

  • BMI of 19-25 is the norm;
  • BMI of 25-30 indicates a "pre-obese" - increased body weight, which is necessary to normalize;
  • BMI of 30-35 corresponds to the first degree of obesity;
  • BMI of 35-40 speaks of the second degree;
  • BMI above 40 indicates the presence of third-degree obesity.

Determining the degree of obesity. Method two

The second method is to determine overweight relative to the norm in percentage is used much less frequently than the first.

If "preponderance" is not more than 10%, it is not obese and completion. With an excess of 10-29% are diagnosed with "1 degree of obesity", with 30-49% - the second degree, at 50% and more - the third degree.
