If you remove the uterus

Many women believe destroy perfectly protected and hidden in the depths of the pelvic muscular organ that during childbirth exerts a force comparable to the thrust of the car in first gear may only some quite terrible disease. In fact it is not. Here they are mortal enemies of the uterus.

Fibroids are large. In this disease, the body size is increased as in pregnancy. And kogdamiomavyrastaet more "12 weeks", the uterus should be removed.

Endometriosis - the expansion of the inner layer of the endometrium. Possible at any age, it provoked abortions and other gynecological surgery. The main danger is that endometriosis can spread to other organs: vagina, ovaries, bladder, rectum.

Treatment of long courses of expensive drugs does not always help.

Cervical cancer occurs in women of all ages, including even the virgins. Scientists are increasingly inclined to believe that this form of cancer linked to the human papilloma virus, which is transmitted through sexual contact. The disease can be easily detected by physical examination and successfully cured in the early stages, but in advanced cases of hysterectomy - the only way out.

Uterine cancer develops mainly between 40 and 50 years. Symptoms vary: weight loss, weakness, pain, and bleeding. The most radical method of treatment - removal of the uterus.

How to live

Behind the operation, life slowly returns to the groove... But this process interfere with heavy thoughts: to tell whether such a delicate operation on her husband? What will happen to the body, forcibly deprived of the uterus, in 2-3 years? And most importantly - has remained a woman a woman, if she can hope for love and happiness?

The same questions once asked experts and obstetrics and gynecology clinics of V. F. Snegiryov MMA behalf IM M. Sechenov. They conducted psychological testing of women who have had a hysterectomy - and that's what the results were.
It turns out that before the operation 80% of women are seriously thinking about the consequences. 39% were not afraid of her, of the remaining 61% quarter was afraid of death, 18% - of complications, 9% - a broken relationship with her husband, 6% - lack of results, 3% - early aging and the same thought about the "cosmetic" side of the issue.

The vast majority - 94% of women - after surgery found that the fears were not realized. One-third of women who have experienced self-doubt, depression. But 38% felt that their quality of life improved significantly after surgery, and 16% even harmonize relations in the family! And basically it was the patients who did not hide the fact from their husbands operations.

The majority of the operated relationships with their husbands remained flat, with only one in ten said the deterioration of family relationships.
